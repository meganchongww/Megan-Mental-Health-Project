Excessive anxiety and worry (apprehensive expectation), occurring more days than not for at least 6 months, about a number of events or activities (such as work or school performance).
Depression is a mood disorder that causes a persistent feeling of sadness and loss of interest for long periods of time.
Eating Disorders are behavioral conditions characterized by severe and persistent disturbance in eating behaviors and associated distressing thoughts and emotions.
Addiction & Substance Abuse is defined as a chronic, relapsing pattern of compulsive substance use despite adverse consequences.
ADHD is marked by an ongoing pattern of inattention and/or hyperactivity-impulsivity that interferes with functioning or development.
PTSD is the development of a set of reactions in people who have experienced a traumatic event that might have threatened their life or safety, or others around them.
OCD is a long-lasting pattern of behaviour in which a person experiences uncontrollable and recurring thoughts (obsessions), engages in repetitive behaviors (compulsions), or both.
Generally affecting people over the age of 65, Dementia is characterised by the progressive worsening of memory and intellect (cognitive abilities), orientation and personality to levels that affect daily life and activities.
